Sam Ticknor is a multidisciplinary artist living in New York City (previously, Pittsburgh, PA). Sam spent many years making art in Pittsburgh at Carnegie Mellon University where she earned a BFA in Fine Art, at the Carnegie Museums of Pittsburgh where she worked as a Creative Technologist, at Union Project where she was a member of the ceramics cooperative, and in her home studio.

These days, Sam is focusing on painting colorful vignettes of the moments that stick in her memory. This body of work is largely figurative, featuring scenes that have one foot in reality and one foot in her imagination. The paintings bear resemblance to American folk art with their invented perspective, whimsical colors, playful storylines, and sense of individuality. The scenery is adapted from Sam's modern life, and steeped in metaphor. Her reverence for life and nature is revealed through tender storytelling about the paintings' subjects. She aim to draw viewers in with curious optical play and entice them to make sense of the narrative. Punchlines can be discovered, they range from comedic to bittersweet to alarming.
