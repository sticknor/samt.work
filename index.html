I enjoy making many kinds of art: painting, drawing, ceramics, hand-drawn animation, computer-generated animation, video games, photography, and collage. I'm always in a rush to make new things. When I see something that sticks to my brain, I have to make something. By the time I [paint] it out of my head, I've already forgot a lot of details. So I make up new characters and lighting and space as I go.

I work at a museum lab, the Innovation Studio. We’re a small team working on interactive projects for the Carnegie Museums of Pittsburgh. I’ve worked on interactive exhibits and apps for the Andy Warhol Museum, Carnegie Museum of Art, and Carnegie Museum of Natural History. I'm particularly proud of the projects included below. In these, I led UX design, software development, and graphic design/illustration. I venture to make software that is as beautiful and physical as a [painting] can be.
Dawn Chorus
2017
Dawn Chorus is an alarm clock application, available on Android and iOS. This app is a calming alternative to your usual blaring alarm. When the alarm goes off, birds join together to create a chorus of song. For further information about this project, see Introducing Dawn Chorus and Evaluating Dawn Chorus.
We Are Nature
2017
In October 2017, the Studio launched three interactive projects in Carnegie Museum of Natural History’s exhibition We Are Nature: Living in the Anthropocene. I was very excited to create the illustrations, and happy to bring them to life with code. For further information about these projects, see the Github repo. You can also read An Experiment In Data Visualization: a blog post about one of the projects which features over 6000 frames of interactive animation.
I like to sell and show my artwork, and teach art workshops.
